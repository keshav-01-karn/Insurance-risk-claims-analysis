## Problem Statement

Insurance companies deal with large volumes of policyholder and claims data, but extracting meaningful insights from this data is crucial for **risk assessment, pricing strategies, and fraud detection**.

This project aims to build an **interactive Power BI dashboard** to analyze insurance data and answer key business questions related to:
- Claim patterns and financial impact  
- Customer risk profiling  
- Policy distribution across demographics and regions  

---

## Business Objective

The goal of this project is to enable data-driven decision-making by:
- Understanding claim behavior and risk factors  
- Identifying high-risk customer segments  
- Analyzing how demographics and vehicle attributes influence claims  
- Supporting better pricing and underwriting strategies

##  Dashboard Features

### 🔹 Claims Analysis
- Total Claim Amount by:
  - Car Use (Private vs Commercial)
  - Car Make (Brand-wise risk)
  - Coverage Zone (Urban vs Rural risk)

### 🔹 Customer Segmentation
- Age Group Analysis → Identifies high-risk age brackets  
- Gender Distribution → Balanced policyholder base  
- Education & Marital Status → Combined behavioral insights  

### 🔹 Risk Factors
- Kids Driving → Higher claims observed with more young drivers  
- Car Year → Older vs newer vehicle risk trends  
- Car Usage → Commercial vehicles show higher claim exposure  

### 🔹 Dynamic Analysis
- Toggle between:
  - **Total Policies**
  - **Total Claim Amount**
- Enables comparative analysis across all visuals

##  Key Insights

Based on dashboard analysis:

-  **Private vehicles contribute the majority of total claims**, but commercial vehicles show higher risk per policy  
-  **Urban and highly urban zones show higher claim amounts**, indicating increased accident probability  
-  **Age group 26–55 contributes the highest claims**, representing the most active driving population  
-  **Households with multiple kids driving show increased claim amounts**, indicating higher risk exposure  
-  **Certain car brands (e.g., Ford, Chevrolet)** have higher claim values, possibly due to usage volume or repair cost  
-  **Balanced gender distribution (~50-50)** suggests equal market penetration
